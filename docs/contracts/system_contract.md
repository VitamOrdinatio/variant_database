# system_contract.md

## Purpose

This document defines the top-level system contract for the Variant Database (VDB).

The system contract establishes the non-negotiable obligations that all VDB implementation work must satisfy.

This contract governs VDB as a preservation-first scientific evidence substrate.

VDB must preserve producer-emitted scientific assertions, maintain reconstructable evidence custody, derive governed evidence organization, expose structurally eligible convergence surfaces, and emit consumer-specific projections without transferring source authority to any derived layer.

# System Role

VDB is the persistent scientific evidence substrate for the VitamOrdinatio repository ecosystem.

VDB must preserve, organize, expose, project, and reconstruct evidence generated by producer repositories.

VDB must support downstream reasoning systems by exposing governed reasoning affordances while preserving the boundary between evidence preservation and biological interpretation.

Initial producer families include:

```text
VAP
GSC
```

Planned or future producer / consumer families include:

```text
RSP
RDGP
TEP-VDB
TEP-RDGP
external evidence capsules
future omics producers
future validation producers
future reasoning producers
```

VDB must support producer evolution without rewriting previously preserved evidence.

# Core System Principle

```text
VDB preserves assertions before it organizes evidence.

VDB organizes evidence before it exposes evidence.

VDB exposes evidence before it projects evidence.

VDB projects evidence before downstream systems reason over evidence.
```

Every implementation decision must protect:

```text
identity
provenance
authority
uncertainty
assertion primacy
evidence topology
convergence geometry
surface eligibility
projection lineage
reasoning currency
future reinterpretability
reconstructability
```

The core authority rule is:

```text
Assertions have preservation authority.

Derived layers add capability.

Derived layers do not acquire source authority.
```

# System Boundary

VDB is responsible for:

```text
reading certified producer TEPs during registration
validating ingestion and registration readiness
registering packages, artifacts, assertions, and source identities
preserving artifacts and evidence states
constructing certified registration units
declaring corpus generations
preserving Assertion Records
brokering namespaces
deriving Evidence Topology
characterizing Convergence Geometry
exposing Evidence Convergence Surfaces
constructing governed Projection Views
emitting RDGP-ready consumer projections
supporting evidence reconstruction
supporting validation and certification
preserving reasoning outputs when returned as producer assertions
```

VDB is not responsible for:

```text
modifying producer artifacts
rerunning producer pipelines
overwriting producer evidence
collapsing source identities into canonical identities
treating topology as source evidence
treating geometry as source evidence
treating surfaces as source evidence
treating projections as source evidence
performing clinical diagnosis
performing RDGP reasoning
declaring biological causality
determining biological truth
determining clinical actionability
```

VDB prepares and exposes preserved evidence structure.

Downstream systems reason over that structure.

When downstream reasoning systems emit new evidence products, those products may re-enter VDB only as new preserved assertions.

# Architectural Layering Obligations

VDB implementation must preserve the following architectural layering:

```text
Assertion Records
        ↓
Evidence Topology
        ↓
Convergence Geometry
        ↓
Evidence Convergence Surfaces
        ↓
Projection Views
        ↓
Downstream Reasoning
```

Each layer has a distinct responsibility.

## Assertion Records

Assertion Records are the primary preserved scientific objects in VDB.

Assertion Records preserve what a producer scientifically claimed, under what context, with what evidence basis, provenance, authority context, and uncertainty context.

Assertion Records are preservation-authoritative objects.

They preserve producer claims.

They do not represent VDB belief.

They are not VDB endorsements of biological correctness.

## Evidence Topology

Evidence Topology is derived organization over preserved Assertion Records.

Topology answers:

```text
What is connected?
```

Evidence Topology must be deterministic, traceable, reconstructable, and derived from preserved assertions.

Topology must not become source evidence.

## Convergence Geometry

Convergence Geometry is derived structural characterization over Evidence Topology.

Geometry answers:

```text
What structural properties emerge from those connections?
```

Convergence Geometry may characterize density, breadth, depth, cross-producer convergence, modality diversity, provenance diversity, epistemic diversity, temporal persistence, or other declared structural features.

Convergence Geometry must not perform biological reasoning.

## Evidence Convergence Surfaces

Evidence Convergence Surfaces are governed exposure objects over Convergence Geometry.

Surfaces expose structurally eligible reasoning capacity.

Surfaces do not determine biological meaning.

Surfaces must preserve eligibility basis, disclosure basis, source geometry, evidence strata, lineage, and generation context.

## Projection Views

Projection Views are purpose-bound representations over preserved assertions or governed derived layers.

Projection changes representation.

Projection does not change authority.

Graphs, tables, matrices, dashboards, TEP-VDB exports, validation reports, and consumer-specific packages are all projection views when derived from VDB-governed sources.

No projection becomes source truth.

## Downstream Reasoning

Downstream systems, including RDGP, consume VDB projections and perform biological reasoning.

Downstream reasoning outputs may re-enter VDB only as new producer assertions.

Returned reasoning assertions must not overwrite the evidence state from which they were derived.

# Layer Authority And Traceability Obligations

Every VDB layer must declare its authority status and derivation basis.

VDB recognizes the following authority structure:

```text
Producer artifacts
    immutable transported source evidence

Registration units
    custody records over accepted producer evidence packages

Assertion Records
    primary preserved scientific claims

Evidence Topology
    derived organization over Assertion Records

Convergence Geometry
    derived structural characterization over Evidence Topology

Evidence Convergence Surfaces
    governed exposure over Convergence Geometry

Projection Views
    purpose-bound representations for consumers, validation, export, or review

Downstream Reasoning
    external interpretation performed outside VDB
```

No downstream layer may acquire the authority of an upstream layer.

Every derived layer must preserve traceability to the layer from which it was derived.

The canonical full Phase 4 derivation chain is:

```text
Projection View
        ↓
Evidence Convergence Surface
        ↓
Convergence Geometry
        ↓
Evidence Topology
        ↓
Assertion Record
        ↓
registration unit
        ↓
producer artifact
        ↓
producer TEP
```

Not every projection is required to include every intermediate derived layer.

A projection may be generated from preserved assertions, registration unit metadata, corpus generation metadata, validation records, topology, geometry, surfaces, or other governed VDB sources.

When a projection does not include the full Phase 4 chain, it must explicitly declare:

```text
projection source layer
projection source records
omitted intermediate layers, if any
reason the omitted layers are not applicable
traceability path to preserved assertions or registration units
```

A derived record is VDB-compliant only if its derivation chain can be reconstructed.

If a derived object cannot be traced back to preserved assertions, registration units, or another declared governed VDB source, it must not be treated as valid VDB evidence.

# Derived Layer Scope Obligations

Derived layers may organize, characterize, expose, summarize, package, or render preserved evidence.

Derived layers must not:

```text
create biological truth
infer causality
determine pathogenicity
perform diagnosis
rank biological importance
erase uncertainty
collapse independent evidence strata
replace producer assertions
rewrite prior evidence generations
```

A derived layer may create a new VDB record.

A derived layer may not create new source authority.

The distinction between:

```text
record creation
```

and

```text
authority creation
```

must remain explicit throughout VDB implementation.

# Registration Unit Obligations

A registration unit is the canonical registered representation of an accepted producer evidence package.

A registration unit must preserve:

```text
TEP package identity
artifact identity
assertion registrations
source identities
producer family
evidence domains
assertion types
surface-relevant roles when declared
authority context
uncertainty context
source namespaces
extraction methods
referential integrity
validation status
certification status when available
```

In the current implementation, a registration unit may be physically represented as a SQLite database.

The physical representation is not the architecture.

Valid future implementations may use:

```text
one database per TEP
one database per producer run
one database per corpus generation
one central relational database
lakehouse-style partitioned files
object store plus indexed metadata
future storage backends
```

Any physical representation is compliant only if registration unit identity, source package identity, assertion identity, provenance, namespace preservation, and reconstruction paths remain intact.

The operational rule for current MARK development is:

```text
1 accepted TEP → 1 certified SQLite registration unit
```

The architectural rule is:

```text
1 accepted TEP → 1 traceable registration unit
```

# Corpus Generation Obligations

A corpus generation is a declared collection of registration units used as the input basis for topology, geometry, surface, and projection derivation.

A corpus generation must preserve:

```text
corpus generation identity

package identifiers
producer families
artifact counts
assertion counts
source identity counts
certification status
inclusion rationale
exclusion rationale when applicable
build timestamp
derivation policy version
```

A corpus generation must not become source truth.

A corpus generation declares the evidence scope from which derived layers are built.

The Phase 4 implementation boundary is:

```text
certified registration units
        ↓
corpus generation
        ↓
Assertion Records
        ↓
Evidence Topology
        ↓
Convergence Geometry
        ↓
Evidence Convergence Surfaces
        ↓
Projection Views
```

Phase 4 builders should consume certified registration units through declared schemas, manifests, and certification metadata.

Phase 4 builders must not bypass registration by parsing raw producer artifacts directly when certified registration units are available.

---

# Phase Boundary Obligations

VDB implementation phases must respect the following boundary:

```text
Phase 3:
    Producer TEPs → registration units

Phase 4:
    registration units → corpus generations → derived evidence structure
```

Phase 4 must not re-solve Phase 3 ingestion unless registration units are unavailable, invalid, uncertified, or explicitly being regenerated.

Certified Phase 3 registration units are valid checkpointed inputs for Phase 4 topology, geometry, surface, and projection development.

Phase 4 must avoid unnecessary duplication of registered source identities unless materialized integration is explicitly required and reconstructable from source registration units.

# Evidence Object And Evidence State Compatibility Rule

Earlier VDB implementation phases may represent preserved evidence using Evidence Objects, Evidence States, and Evidence State Transitions.

These domains remain valid custody, persistence, and lifecycle implementation domains.

However, after Phase 4 architecture freeze, Evidence Objects and Evidence States must be interpreted as implementation-supporting preservation records rather than the governing scientific primitive.

The governing scientific primitive is the Assertion Record.

Evidence Objects may support:

```text
artifact-level evidence custody
source entity registration
evidence-bearing unit registration
producer-family evidence grouping
artifact-to-assertion traceability
```

Evidence States may support:

```text
lifecycle state tracking
registration state
persistence state
namespace state
overlay attachment state
surface exposure state
projection state
returned reasoning state
```

Evidence State Transitions may support:

```text
state history
custody transitions
registration transitions
namespace transitions
derived-layer exposure transitions
returned producer assertion transitions
```

Evidence Objects, Evidence States, and Evidence State Transitions must not supersede Assertion Records.

They must not become biological conclusions.

They must remain reconstructable, additive, and subordinate to preserved assertion custody.

# Summary

The VDB system contract exists to protect the core mission of the repository.

VDB must preserve scientific assertions such that future systems can identify, trust, organize, characterize, expose, project, reason over, and reconstruct evidence without collapsing its source identity or authority.

The guiding rule is:

```text
Preserve assertions.

Derive organization.

Characterize structure.

Expose surfaces.

Project views.

Never transfer authority upward.
```

Or, operationally:

```text
Preserve broadly.

Expose narrowly.

Project safely.

Never collapse the evidence.
```
