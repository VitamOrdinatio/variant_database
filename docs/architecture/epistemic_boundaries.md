## Purpose

The Truth Layer Philosophy defines what truth means within VDB.

The Scientific Evidence Preservation Principles define what scientific information must remain preserved.

The Evidence Persistence Philosophy defines how VDB preserves that information.

This document defines the limits of VDB's authority.

Its purpose is to establish what VDB knows, what it may legitimately claim, and what it deliberately refuses to conclude.

The integrity of the Truth Layer depends as much upon what VDB does **not** claim as upon what it preserves.

---

# Governing Principle

VDB preserves scientific evidence.

VDB organizes scientific evidence.

VDB exposes scientific evidence.

VDB does not determine biological truth.

---

# Knowledge Within VDB

Knowledge within VDB exists in four progressively richer forms.

```text
Producer Assertions
        ↓
Preserved Evidence
        ↓
Deterministic Evidence Structure
        ↓
Evidence Convergence Surfaces
```

Each layer is derived deterministically from the previous layer.

These remain observational objects.

Biological interpretation begins only after these layers have been constructed.

---

# What VDB Knows

VDB may know:

* which producer emitted an assertion
* which participants appear within an assertion
* which evidence supports an assertion
* which assertions share participants
* which assertions intersect through preserved relationships
* which assertions belong to common evidence topology
* where evidence convergence occurs
* how evidence is connected
* how evidence evolved through time
* how evidence can be reconstructed

These are deterministic properties of preserved scientific evidence.

They do not require biological interpretation.

---

# What VDB Does Not Know

VDB does not know:

* biological causality
* disease mechanisms
* clinical diagnoses
* statistical significance
* mechanistic explanations
* biological correctness
* therapeutic recommendations
* clinical actionability
* experimental validity beyond preserved provenance
* future scientific truth

These questions belong to downstream reasoning systems, scientific investigation, clinical expertise, and experimental validation.

VDB intentionally remains silent where preservation ends and interpretation begins.

---

# Preservation Does Not Imply Agreement

VDB may preserve assertions made by many independent producers.

Those assertions may:

* agree
* disagree
* overlap
* conflict
* evolve over time

Preservation should never be interpreted as endorsement.

A preserved assertion remains the responsibility of its producer.

VDB preserves the existence of the assertion.

It does not adopt the assertion as its own.

---

# Derived Structure Does Not Create New Evidence

Evidence topology is derived.

Convergence geometry is derived.

Evidence convergence surfaces are derived.

These derived structures reveal organization within preserved evidence.

They do not create additional scientific evidence.

Likewise, the presence of convergence does not imply biological significance.

Convergence identifies regions worthy of investigation.

It does not determine what those regions mean.

---

# Observational Boundaries

VDB may legitimately state:

* these assertions share participants
* these assertions originate from independent producers
* these assertions converge within preserved topology
* this convergence surface contains evidence from multiple modalities
* these assertions remain traceable to preserved provenance

These are observational statements.

They describe preserved evidence without extending beyond it.

---

# Inferential Boundaries

VDB must not state:

* this convergence demonstrates causality
* this evidence proves a mechanism
* this patient has disease X
* this interaction explains phenotype Y
* this convergence is statistically enriched
* this hypothesis is biologically correct

Such statements require reasoning beyond preserved evidence.

They therefore fall outside the authority of VDB.

---

# The Boundary Between VDB and RDGP

VDB and RDGP occupy complementary but distinct epistemic roles.

VDB asks:

```text
Where does preserved evidence converge?
```

RDGP asks:

```text
Does this convergence appear biologically meaningful?
```

VDB exposes candidate investigation surfaces.

RDGP evaluates those surfaces using statistical methods, probabilistic reasoning, biological knowledge, and future analytical frameworks.

This separation preserves the integrity of both systems.

---

# The Courtroom Analogy

VDB functions as the evidence docket rather than the court.

It preserves:

* exhibits
* testimony
* chain of custody
* provenance
* relationships among evidence

It organizes the evidence so that investigators can examine it.

It does not deliver the verdict.

Scientific interpretation remains the responsibility of downstream reasoning and human investigation.

---

# Downstream Knowledge Returns as New Evidence

Future reasoning systems may generate:

* statistical models
* confidence estimates
* biological hypotheses
* mechanistic interpretations
* prioritized candidate genes
* causal proposals

These products may later become valuable scientific evidence.

When preserved within VDB, they enter as **new producer assertions** carrying their own:

* producer identity
* provenance
* context
* epistemic status
* evidence basis
* temporal scope

They do not modify previously preserved assertions.

Scientific understanding grows by adding evidence rather than rewriting evidence.

---

# Boundary Violations

The following architectural behaviors violate the Truth Layer.

* Rewriting preserved assertions after downstream reasoning.
* Converting derived structure into preserved evidence.
* Collapsing observational evidence and inferred knowledge.
* Removing producer authority.
* Promoting statistical inference into observational truth.
* Treating convergence as proof.
* Treating representation as evidence.
* Allowing downstream interpretation to alter historical records.

Such violations compromise the scientific integrity of the repository.

---

# Why These Boundaries Matter

Scientific discovery depends upon disciplined separation between observation and interpretation.

Preserved evidence should remain available to future investigators regardless of how scientific understanding changes.

By refusing to exceed its epistemic authority, VDB remains trustworthy.

Its value arises not from claiming more than it knows, but from preserving evidence faithfully enough that future reasoning systems can ask better questions.

The Truth Layer therefore protects scientific progress by protecting epistemic humility.

---

# Summary

The epistemic authority of VDB may be summarized as follows:

```text
VDB preserves.

VDB organizes.

VDB reconstructs.

VDB exposes.

VDB does not conclude.
```

These boundaries ensure that VDB remains a deterministic, provenance-preserving scientific substrate upon which future reasoning systems can safely build without compromising the integrity of the preserved evidence.
