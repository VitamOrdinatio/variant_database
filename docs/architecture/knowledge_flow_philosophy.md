## Purpose

The Truth Layer Philosophy defines what truth means within VDB.

The Scientific Evidence Preservation Principles define what scientific information must remain preserved.

The Evidence Persistence Philosophy defines how preserved evidence is stewarded.

The Epistemic Boundaries define the limits of VDB's authority.

This document defines how scientific knowledge is permitted to flow through the repository ecosystem.

Its purpose is to ensure that scientific understanding grows through accumulation rather than replacement, allowing discovery to emerge without compromising the integrity of preserved evidence.

---

# Governing Principle

Knowledge within VDB does not circulate.

Knowledge expands through accumulation.

Every return to the preservation layer represents the arrival of new scientific evidence rather than revision of historical evidence.

Scientific understanding therefore advances by expanding the preserved scientific record rather than modifying it.

Every transformation enriches scientific understanding while preserving the evidence that enabled it.

Scientific progress therefore emerges through monotonic expansion of preserved knowledge rather than recursive modification of historical evidence.

---

# Knowledge Flow Is Not Data Flow

This document concerns the flow of scientific knowledge rather than the movement of files or database records.

Files may be copied.

Artifacts may be transported.

Tables may be reorganized.

Those implementation details are secondary.

The architectural concern is how scientific understanding evolves while preserving the integrity of the evidentiary record.

Knowledge flow is therefore an epistemic concept rather than a computational one.

---

# The Knowledge Pipeline

Scientific knowledge progresses through a series of unidirectional transformations.

```text
Observation
        ↓
Producer Assertion
        ↓
Evidence Preservation
        ↓
Evidence Persistence
        ↓
Assertion Projection
        ↓
Evidence Topology
        ↓
Convergence Geometry
        ↓
Evidence Convergence Surfaces
        ↓
Statistical Reasoning
        ↓
Biological Interpretation
```

Each stage builds deterministically upon the previous stage.

No stage replaces, edits, or invalidates upstream knowledge.

---

# Monotonic Knowledge Expansion

Knowledge may become:

* richer
* broader
* more connected
* more reproducible
* better understood

Knowledge may not become richer by rewriting preserved scientific evidence.

Scientific understanding expands by adding new evidence and constructing new deterministic representations over existing evidence.

Historical observations remain preserved.

Future understanding grows around them.

---

# Knowledge Conservation

Every architectural transformation should conserve scientific meaning.

Derived representations may reorganize evidence.

They may expose relationships.

They may reveal convergence.

They may improve discoverability.

They must preserve sufficient information that the original scientific meaning remains reconstructable.

Knowledge is therefore conserved even as organization becomes increasingly sophisticated.

---

# Deterministic Transformation

Each architectural layer performs a deterministic transformation.

```text
Assertion
        ↓
Projection
        ↓
Topology
        ↓
Geometry
        ↓
Discovery Surface
```

Each transformation produces additional scientific structure without altering the preserved assertions from which that structure was derived.

Determinism ensures that identical preserved evidence always produces identical derived representations.

---

# Downstream Never Rewrites Upstream

Downstream systems may discover new knowledge.

They may generate:

* hypotheses
* statistical models
* confidence estimates
* biological interpretations
* mechanistic explanations

These discoveries must never modify previously preserved assertions.

Instead, downstream knowledge becomes additional scientific evidence.

Historical evidence remains unchanged.

Scientific understanding grows by accumulation.

---

# New Knowledge Becomes New Evidence

Reasoning systems such as RDGP may produce scientifically valuable conclusions.

Those conclusions may later be preserved within VDB.

When this occurs, they enter the repository ecosystem as new producer assertions carrying their own:

* producer identity
* evidence basis
* provenance
* context
* epistemic status
* temporal scope

They become additional observations within the preserved scientific record.

They do not replace earlier observations.

---

# Knowledge Does Not Loop

The repository ecosystem permits repeated scientific investigation.

It does not permit recursive modification of preserved evidence.

Conceptually, the process resembles a staircase rather than a loop.

```text
Observation
        ↓
Assertion
        ↓
Preservation
        ↓
Discovery
        ↓
Reasoning
        ↓
New Assertion
        ↓
Preservation
        ↓
Discovery
```

Each return to preservation represents the arrival of new scientific evidence rather than revision of existing evidence.

The scientific record therefore grows upward rather than cycling backward.

---

# Producer Ecosystem Evolution

As new repositories are introduced into the ecosystem, they participate in the same architectural flow.

Future producers may include:

* additional omics repositories
* clinical repositories
* imaging repositories
* environmental evidence systems
* machine learning systems
* future reasoning engines

Every producer contributes new assertions.

No producer acquires authority to rewrite previously preserved evidence.

This principle allows the ecosystem to expand indefinitely while maintaining architectural consistency.

---

# Discovery Emerges From Accumulation

Discovery is not inserted into the architecture.

It emerges naturally from preserved evidence.

As evidence accumulates:

* topology becomes richer
* convergence becomes denser
* discovery surfaces become more informative
* downstream reasoning gains additional statistical power

The Discovery Engine therefore depends upon disciplined evidence accumulation rather than speculative inference.

---

# Scientific Progress

Scientific understanding evolves because investigators repeatedly revisit preserved evidence using improved methods.

The preserved evidence remains constant.

The methods improve.

The mathematical frameworks evolve.

The questions become richer.

Knowledge flow therefore enables scientific progress without requiring historical reconstruction or reinterpretation of preserved observations.

---

# Architectural Consequences

The knowledge flow architecture of VDB exhibits several defining characteristics.

It is:

* unidirectional
* deterministic
* monotonic
* append-only
* provenance-preserving
* reconstruction-oriented
* discovery-enabling
* producer-respecting
* future-compatible

These characteristics ensure that future scientific advances enrich the preserved record rather than replacing it.

---

# Relationship to Future Discovery

The Discovery Engine is one consequence of this architectural philosophy.

As scientific evidence accumulates across producers, preserved assertions organize into increasingly rich evidence topology.

That topology gives rise to convergence geometry.

Convergence geometry gives rise to evidence convergence surfaces.

Those surfaces provide future reasoning systems with increasingly informative regions for statistical investigation.

Knowledge therefore becomes progressively more discoverable without compromising the integrity of preserved evidence.

---

# Summary

Scientific progress depends upon preserving yesterday's observations while allowing tomorrow's understanding to grow.

VDB therefore treats knowledge as an accumulating scientific substrate.

Evidence is preserved.

Structure is derived.

Discovery emerges.

Reasoning adds new evidence.

The scientific record expands without rewriting its own history.

In this way, knowledge within VDB does not circulate.

It advances through monotonic accumulation, ensuring that each generation of scientific investigation inherits a richer evidentiary foundation than the one before it.
