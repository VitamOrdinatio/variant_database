# Purpose

Epoch I established that the immutable Assertion Record is the fundamental preserved scientific object within VDB.

Epoch II asks a different question.

> **Once assertions exist, how do they organize into larger scientific structures?**

This document defines the first deterministic organizational layer above preserved Assertion Records.

It does **not** introduce biological reasoning.

It introduces deterministic evidence organization.

---

# Governing Principle

Evidence Topology is the deterministic organization of preserved Assertion Records.

It is **derived**.

It is **reconstructable**.

It is **non-authoritative**.

Evidence Topology does not replace preserved assertions.

It organizes them.

---

# Relationship to Epoch I

The Truth Layer established:

```text
Observation
        ↓
Producer Assertion
        ↓
Evidence Preservation
        ↓
Evidence Persistence
```

Evidence Topology begins only after preserved assertions already exist.

Accordingly:

```text
Assertion Records
        ↓
Evidence Topology
```

Evidence Topology never creates scientific evidence.

It creates deterministic organization over preserved scientific evidence.

---

# Topology Is Not Evidence

Assertions are preserved scientific evidence.

Evidence Topology is organization over preserved assertions.

This distinction is fundamental.

Topology does not possess independent scientific authority.

Every topological relationship must remain traceable to the preserved Assertion Records from which it was derived.

If the topology is discarded, it must be reproducible entirely from preserved assertions.

---

# Why Topology Rather Than Graphs

Evidence Topology deliberately avoids defining scientific organization in terms of graphs.

Graphs are one possible mathematical representation.

Scientific evidence frequently contains relationships involving multiple simultaneous participants, including:

* samples
* variants
* genes
* phenotypes
* publications
* experimental conditions
* semantic priors
* transcriptomic evidence
* environmental exposures
* future evidence modalities

These relationships are naturally higher-order.

Evidence Topology therefore defines the organizational substrate independently of any specific mathematical representation.

Graphs, hypergraphs, simplicial complexes, tensors, relational projections, and future mathematical frameworks all become possible representations of the same preserved topology.

---

# Inputs

Evidence Topology is derived exclusively from preserved Assertion Records.

Each Assertion Record contributes organizational information through its preserved components, including:

* participants
* relationships
* context
* provenance
* evidence basis
* producer identity
* epistemic status
* temporal scope
* independence metadata

No additional biological interpretation is introduced during topology construction.

# Topological Dimensions

Evidence Topology organizes Assertion Records across multiple deterministic dimensions.

These dimensions include, but are not limited to:

## Participant Topology

Organization based upon shared biological or scientific participants.

Examples:

* sample
* variant
* gene
* phenotype
* transcript
* pathway
* publication

---

## Relationship Topology

Organization based upon shared assertion relationships.

Examples:

* contains
* supports
* annotates
* prioritizes
* differentially_expressed
* associated_with

---

## Context Topology

Organization according to shared biological or analytical context.

Examples include:

* phenotype
* disease
* tissue
* experimental contrast
* sequencing run
* environmental condition
* annotation release

---

## Provenance Topology

Organization according to shared scientific origin.

Examples include:

* producer repository
* source publication
* source database
* execution run
* transport envelope
* lineage

---

## Epistemic Topology

Organization according to preserved knowledge class.

Examples include:

* observed
* annotated
* asserted
* brokered
* derived
* inferred
* hypothesized

---

## Temporal Topology

Organization according to preserved historical progression.

Examples include:

* release chronology
* annotation version
* producer evolution
* historical evidence accumulation

---

## Independence Topology

Organization according to independent evidence origin.

Assertions derived from the same underlying evidence source should remain distinguishable from assertions originating from independent evidence streams.

Preserving evidence independence enables downstream statistical reasoning without conflating duplicated evidence with independent corroboration.

# Higher-Order Organization

Evidence Topology naturally supports organizational structures extending beyond pairwise relationships.

Examples include:

* assertion neighborhoods
* participant neighborhoods
* cross-modal evidence neighborhoods
* multi-participant organizational structures
* producer-crossing evidence structures
* phenotype-centered organizational regions

These higher-order structures remain deterministic consequences of preserved assertions.

They introduce no new scientific claims.

---

# Deterministic Reconstruction

Evidence Topology must remain fully reconstructable.

Given a preserved corpus of Assertion Records, identical topological organization should always be reproduced.

This property ensures:

* reproducibility
* traceability
* verification
* future compatibility

Topology therefore remains a derived artifact rather than an independently maintained scientific object.

---

# What Evidence Topology May Claim

Evidence Topology may legitimately describe deterministic organizational properties such as:

* these assertions share participant POLG
* these assertions share epilepsy context
* these assertions originate from independent producers
* these assertions intersect through common provenance
* these assertions participate in the same assertion neighborhood
* these assertions connect multiple evidence modalities

These are organizational observations derived from preserved evidence.

---

# What Evidence Topology May Not Claim

Evidence Topology must not conclude:

* biological causality
* disease mechanism
* statistical enrichment
* clinical significance
* diagnostic certainty
* therapeutic recommendation
* biological correctness

These conclusions belong to downstream reasoning systems.

Topology remains observational.

---

# Architectural Consequences

Evidence Topology establishes several architectural invariants.

* Assertion Records remain primary.
* Topology remains derived.
* Organization remains deterministic.
* Every relationship remains traceable.
* Reconstruction is always possible.
* Multiple mathematical representations remain valid.
* Biological interpretation remains downstream.

These invariants ensure that future analytical methods may evolve without requiring redesign of the preserved scientific substrate.

---

# Summary

Evidence Topology is the first organizational layer built upon preserved Assertion Records.

It transforms preserved scientific evidence into deterministic organizational structure without altering scientific meaning.

Assertions remain the authoritative preserved scientific objects.

Topology provides their organization.

Future mathematical representations may change.

Future reasoning methods may evolve.

The preserved assertions remain constant, while Evidence Topology provides the stable organizational substrate from which Convergence Geometry, Discovery Surfaces, and future scientific investigation naturally emerge.
