# Purpose

Evidence Topology organizes preserved Assertion Records into deterministic organizational relationships.

Convergence Geometry builds upon that organization.

Its purpose is to characterize the structural properties that emerge within Evidence Topology without introducing biological interpretation.

Convergence Geometry is therefore the second organizational abstraction of Epoch II.

---

# Governing Principle

Convergence Geometry is the deterministic characterization of structure within Evidence Topology.

It is:

* derived
* deterministic
* reconstructable
* non-authoritative

Convergence Geometry does not create scientific evidence.

It characterizes the organization of preserved scientific evidence.

---

# Relationship to Evidence Topology

Evidence Topology answers:

> **What is connected?**

Convergence Geometry answers:

> **What structural properties emerge from those connections?**

Conceptually:

```text
Assertion Records
        ↓
Evidence Topology
        ↓
Convergence Geometry
```

Assertions remain the authoritative scientific objects.

Topology organizes assertions.

Geometry characterizes topology.

---

# Geometry Is Not Interpretation

Convergence Geometry must remain observational.

It may describe structural properties.

It must not assign biological meaning.

Geometry therefore does not determine:

* causality
* disease mechanism
* statistical significance
* clinical relevance
* therapeutic value
* biological correctness

Those responsibilities belong to downstream reasoning systems.

---

# Convergence Regions

Convergence Geometry introduces the concept of a **Convergence Region**.

A Convergence Region is a bounded portion of Evidence Topology in which multiple deterministic organizational relationships intersect.

A Convergence Region is not a biological conclusion.

It is simply a region of preserved evidence organization worthy of structural characterization.

Examples might include regions centered around:

* a shared gene
* a shared phenotype
* a shared variant
* a shared biological pathway
* a recurring combination of participants
* a cross-modal evidence intersection

The existence of a region carries no implication regarding biological importance.

---

# Geometry Features

Each Convergence Region possesses structural characteristics.

These characteristics describe the geometry of the evidence rather than its biological meaning.

Examples include:

* organizational density
* structural breadth
* structural depth
* intersection complexity
* producer diversity
* modality diversity
* epistemic diversity
* provenance diversity
* evidence independence
* temporal persistence
* temporal accumulation

These features describe how evidence is organized.

They do not explain why it exists.

---

# Structural Motifs

Certain organizational structures may recur throughout the topology.

These recurring organizational patterns are termed **Structural Motifs**.

Examples include:

* repeated participant intersections
* recurring phenotype-centered organization
* repeated cross-producer organization
* recurring multi-participant structures
* repeated provenance structures
* recurring epistemic organization

Structural motifs are deterministic observations.

They are not biological hypotheses.

---

# Convergence Profiles

Every Convergence Region may be summarized by a Convergence Profile.

A Convergence Profile describes the structural characteristics of that region.

A profile may summarize:

* participant composition
* producer composition
* modality composition
* provenance diversity
* epistemic diversity
* independence characteristics
* temporal characteristics
* organizational complexity

Profiles exist to characterize evidence organization.

They do not rank or interpret it.

---

# Structural Dimensions

Convergence Geometry characterizes organization across multiple structural dimensions.

These dimensions include:

## Density

How extensively assertions organize within a region.

---

## Intersection

The extent to which organizational relationships overlap.

---

## Breadth

The diversity of participating evidence components.

---

## Depth

The degree of organizational layering within a region.

---

## Producer Diversity

The number and diversity of independent producer families contributing to the region.

---

## Modality Diversity

The diversity of biological evidence modalities represented.

---

## Provenance Diversity

The diversity of independent scientific origins contributing to the region.

---

## Epistemic Diversity

The diversity of preserved knowledge classes represented.

---

## Independence

The extent to which organizational support derives from independent evidence origins.

---

## Temporal Persistence

The extent to which organizational structure remains stable across time and evolving scientific knowledge.

# Mathematical Independence

Convergence Geometry intentionally avoids dependence upon any specific mathematical framework.

The preserved geometric structure may later be represented or analyzed using:

* relational methods
* graph methods
* hypergraphs
* simplicial complexes
* persistent homology
* network motifs
* community analysis
* tensor representations
* future mathematical frameworks

The geometry remains the scientific object.

The mathematical representation remains replaceable.

# What Convergence Geometry May Claim

Convergence Geometry may legitimately describe observations such as:

* this region spans multiple producer families
* this region contains multiple independent evidence origins
* this region exhibits recurring organizational motifs
* this region connects multiple biological modalities
* this region demonstrates persistent organizational structure
* this region bridges multiple topology dimensions

These statements remain purely structural.

---

# What Convergence Geometry May Not Claim

Convergence Geometry must not conclude:

* biological causation
* pathogenicity
* statistical enrichment
* disease mechanism
* clinical actionability
* hypothesis confirmation
* scientific correctness

Those conclusions belong to downstream reasoning systems.

---

# Relationship to Discovery

Convergence Geometry is an internal organizational layer.

It prepares preserved evidence for later discovery.

Conceptually:

```text
Evidence Topology
        ↓
Convergence Geometry
        ↓
Evidence Convergence Surfaces
```

Geometry characterizes organizational structure.

The Discovery Layer later determines which structural regions become queryable Evidence Convergence Surfaces.

---

# Architectural Consequences

Convergence Geometry establishes several architectural invariants.

* Geometry is derived from Evidence Topology.
* Geometry remains reconstructable.
* Geometry preserves assertion authority.
* Geometry preserves producer authority.
* Geometry introduces no biological interpretation.
* Geometry remains mathematically representation-independent.
* Geometry serves future discovery rather than replacing scientific reasoning.

These invariants preserve the separation between deterministic organization and downstream inference.

---

# Summary

Convergence Geometry is the structural characterization layer of the Evidence Geometry epoch.

It transforms deterministic organizational relationships into structural descriptions without altering scientific meaning.

Assertions remain the preserved scientific record.

Evidence Topology organizes those assertions.

Convergence Geometry characterizes that organization.

Only after these layers exist may the Discovery Layer expose regions of organizational interest for downstream investigation and statistical reasoning.

In this way, VDB continues to preserve scientific evidence while remaining agnostic to both current and future analytical methods.
