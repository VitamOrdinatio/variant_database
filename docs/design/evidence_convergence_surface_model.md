# Purpose

Epoch I established that VDB preserves scientific assertions without interpreting them.

Epoch II established that preserved assertions can be organized into Evidence Topology and structurally characterized through Convergence Geometry.

Epoch III asks the next architectural question:

> **How do structurally characterized regions of preserved evidence become safely discoverable?**

This document defines the Evidence Convergence Surface as the governed exposure layer between VDB's internal evidence geometry and downstream reasoning systems.

An Evidence Convergence Surface exposes deterministic reasoning capacity.

It does not perform reasoning.

---

# Governing Principle

An Evidence Convergence Surface is a governed disclosure interface that exposes structurally eligible Convergence Geometry as deterministic reasoning capacity for downstream systems.

It is:

* geometry-derived
* structurally eligible
* disclosure-governed
* traceable
* reconstructable
* non-authoritative
* non-interpretive

A surface exposes an opportunity for investigation.

It does not determine the meaning of that opportunity.

---

# Relationship to Prior Layers

The Discovery Layer inherits the deterministic chain established by Epochs I and II.

```text
Assertion Records
        ↓
Evidence Topology
        ↓
Convergence Geometry
        ↓
Evidence Convergence Surfaces
        ↓
Downstream Statistical Reasoning
```

Each layer has a distinct responsibility.

| Layer                         | Responsibility |
| ----------------------------- | -------------- |
| Assertion Records             | Preserve       |
| Evidence Topology             | Organize       |
| Convergence Geometry          | Characterize   |
| Evidence Convergence Surfaces | Expose         |
| RDGP / downstream reasoning   | Evaluate       |
| Scientists / clinicians       | Interpret      |

Evidence Convergence Surfaces must not collapse these responsibilities.

---

# Definition

An Evidence Convergence Surface is a bounded, queryable, governed disclosure view over one or more Convergence Regions that satisfy structural eligibility criteria for downstream investigation.

A surface is not a biological conclusion.

A surface is not a statistical result.

A surface is not a hypothesis.

A surface is an exposure boundary.

It defines what VDB can safely disclose to downstream reasoning systems while preserving provenance, traceability, epistemic status, and reconstruction paths.

---

# Surface Is Not Evidence

Assertions are preserved evidence.

Evidence Topology is organization over preserved assertions.

Convergence Geometry is structural characterization over topology.

Evidence Convergence Surface is exposure over geometry.

Accordingly:

```text
Surface
    ≠
Evidence
```

and

```text
Surface
    ≠
Interpretation
```

A surface may expose evidence context.

It does not become the evidence context.

A surface may expose structural convergence.

It does not explain why convergence exists.

---

# Surface vs. Convergence Region

A Convergence Region is an internal geometry object.

An Evidence Convergence Surface is a governed exposure object.

```text
Convergence Region
        internal structural characterization

Evidence Convergence Surface
        governed queryable disclosure over one or more regions
```

A region exists because Convergence Geometry characterized a bounded area of Evidence Topology.

A surface exists because that region satisfies deterministic exposure rules.

This distinction is critical.

Regions characterize structure.

Surfaces expose structure.

---

# Discovery as Exposure

The Discovery Layer does not discover biological meaning.

Instead, it exposes structurally eligible regions of evidence geometry.

This means VDB may say:

> This surface exposes a geometry-derived convergence region satisfying eligibility rule X.

VDB may not say:

> This convergence is biologically important.

Discovery, within VDB, means governed discoverability.

It does not mean interpretation.

---

# Reasoning Capacity

Evidence Convergence Surfaces expose deterministic reasoning capacity.

Reasoning capacity means the maximum evidence context VDB can safely expose for downstream reasoning under a governed disclosure rule.

VDB defines the possible investigation space.

RDGP or another downstream system evaluates within that space.

Conceptually:

```text
VDB exposes deterministic reasoning capacity.
RDGP evaluates a subset of that capacity.
RDGP returns reasoning assertions.
VDB preserves those assertions.
```

VDB therefore sets the upper bound on what downstream systems can evaluate.

It does not determine which exposed regions will survive downstream reasoning.

---

# Surface Eligibility

A surface must declare why it is eligible for exposure.

This is the role of the surface eligibility basis.

The surface eligibility basis answers:

> **Why may this Convergence Geometry become an exposed surface?**

Examples of surface eligibility concepts include:

* cross-producer geometry
* multi-modal geometry
* independent-evidence geometry
* epistemic-diversity geometry
* temporal-persistence geometry
* multi-component geometry
* audit-requested geometry
* reasoning-reentry geometry

Eligibility is structural.

It is not biological.

A surface is eligible because it satisfies deterministic structural criteria, not because VDB considers it important.

---

# Surface Disclosure

A surface must also declare why specific information is disclosed.

This is the role of the surface disclosure basis.

The surface disclosure basis answers:

> **Why are these specific evidence elements exposed to the downstream consumer?**

Examples of disclosure concepts include:

* minimum reasoning context
* assertion lineage required
* topology lineage required
* geometry feature required
* provenance audit required
* epistemic status required
* independence context required
* temporal context required
* consumer contract required

Eligibility determines whether a surface may exist.

Disclosure determines what the surface contains.

These two concepts must remain distinct.

---

# Governed Disclosure Contract

An Evidence Convergence Surface functions as a governed disclosure contract between VDB and downstream reasoning systems.

It exposes:

* enough information for reasoning
* enough provenance for audit
* enough lineage for reconstruction
* enough context for interpretation by downstream systems

It does not expose arbitrary internal state.

It does not flatten provenance.

It does not discard traceability.

It does not editorialize.

The surface defines the reasoning context VDB is willing to disclose without violating the Truth Layer.

---

# Surface Contents

An Evidence Convergence Surface may expose:

* surface identity
* surface generation
* surface eligibility basis
* surface disclosure basis
* surface evidence strata
* region anchors
* source Convergence Regions
* Geometry Features
* Structural Motifs
* Convergence Profiles
* source topology relationships
* source Assertion Records
* producer identities
* modality context
* provenance context
* epistemic status
* independence context
* temporal context
* reasoning currency state
* refresh state
* downstream handoff references

These contents are exposed as traceable context.

They are not exposed as biological conclusions.

---

# Evidence Strata

Evidence Convergence Surfaces may contain multiple evidence strata.

A stratum is a preserved class of evidence distinguished by producer, modality, epistemic status, and derivation.

Examples include:

* raw variant observation stratum
* semantic prior stratum
* transcriptomic observation stratum
* proteomic observation stratum
* clinical observation stratum
* reasoning-output stratum
* validation stratum

Surfaces may compose multiple strata.

They must not homogenize them.

A surface may expose variant evidence, semantic prior evidence, transcriptomic evidence, proteomic evidence, and RDGP reasoning assertions together.

It must preserve that these are different kinds of evidence.

---

# Composite Surfaces

A composite surface exposes more than one evidence stratum.

Composite surfaces enable cross-modal and reasoning-informed investigation while preserving evidence type distinctions.

For example, a future surface may expose convergence among:

* VAP variant assertions
* GSC semantic prior assertions
* RSP transcriptomic assertions
* PTN proteomic assertions
* RDGP reasoning assertions

VDB may expose that these strata intersect structurally.

VDB must not claim that one stratum confirms another.

Confirmation, weighting, contradiction, and interpretation belong downstream.

---

# Surface Generations

Evidence Convergence Surfaces are generation-aware.

A surface generation reflects the VDB evidentiary state from which the surface was produced.

Conceptually:

```text
VDB generation 1
        ↓
Surface generation 1
        ↓
RDGP reasoning
        ↓
TEP-RDGP
        ↓
VDB generation 2
        ↓
Surface generation 2
```

Each generation expands the preserved scientific record.

It does not rewrite prior generations.

A later surface may expose additional reasoning capacity because new assertions have entered VDB.

Earlier surfaces remain historically valid relative to the evidence corpus from which they were generated.

---

# Generational Surface Expansion

Evidence Convergence Surfaces may expand across generations.

A first-generation surface may expose deterministic geometry derived only from raw producer assertions.

A downstream reasoning engine may evaluate that surface and emit TEP-RDGP.

When VDB ingests that TEP-RDGP, RDGP outputs become new preserved assertions.

Future topology and geometry may incorporate those reasoning assertions.

Later-generation surfaces may therefore expose reasoning-informed evidence geometry.

This remains safe because RDGP outputs enter VDB as new assertions rather than modifications to existing assertions.

---

# Reasoning-Informed Surfaces

A reasoning-informed surface is a surface whose exposed geometry includes preserved downstream reasoning assertions.

Reasoning-informed surfaces may expose that RDGP or another downstream system produced assertions over an earlier surface.

They must preserve:

* reasoning producer identity
* reasoning method context
* input surface lineage
* input evidence corpus
* model or method version
* run provenance
* epistemic status
* confidence or support metadata where available

VDB may expose reasoning-informed structure.

VDB may not adopt reasoning conclusions as its own.

---

# Reasoning Re-Entry

Downstream reasoning outputs may re-enter VDB through TEP-RDGP or future reasoning transport packages.

When this occurs, they are preserved as new producer assertions.

They may then participate in future topology, geometry, and surface exposure.

This process is not recursion.

It is monotonic expansion.

```text
Surface
        ↓
Reasoning
        ↓
New Assertion
        ↓
Preservation
        ↓
Future Surface
```

The earlier evidence remains unchanged.

The later evidence corpus becomes richer.

---

# Reasoning Currency

Reasoning-informed surfaces are valid only relative to the evidence corpus and reasoning method used to generate them.

A reasoning-informed surface must therefore preserve reasoning currency.

Reasoning currency answers:

> **Is the reasoning associated with this surface current relative to the relevant evidence corpus and declared reasoning method?**

A reasoning-informed surface may become stale for two broad reasons:

1. new raw evidence enters VDB
2. the downstream reasoning method changes

Both cases require explicit tracking.

---

# Evidence-Triggered Refresh

A new raw producer TEP may enter VDB after a reasoning-informed surface has already been generated.

Examples include:

* new proteomics evidence
* new transcriptomics evidence
* new clinical evidence
* new imaging evidence
* new environmental evidence
* new literature-derived evidence

When this occurs, the raw evidence corpus changes.

Any existing reasoning-informed surface computed before the new evidence arrived may no longer represent reasoning over the current raw evidence state.

The surface remains preserved.

Its reasoning currency changes.

VDB may mark refresh as warranted.

---

# Reasoning-Method-Triggered Refresh

A reasoning-informed surface may also become stale even when no new raw evidence arrives.

This can occur when a downstream reasoning system changes:

* model version
* statistical method
* calibration scheme
* null model
* evidence weighting strategy
* inference framework
* validation regime

VDB does not need to understand the mathematical superiority of the new method.

It only needs to know that the declared current reasoning method differs from the method used to generate existing reasoning assertions.

This is a metadata fact.

It is not a biological interpretation.

---

# Reasoning Parity

A surface has reasoning parity when downstream reasoning assertions have been generated using the current relevant evidence corpus and the current declared reasoning method.

When new evidence enters or reasoning methods change, parity may be broken.

When downstream reasoning is refreshed and re-ingested, parity may be restored.

Reasoning parity ensures that VDB does not accidentally expose new raw evidence alongside stale reasoning outputs as though the reasoning had considered that evidence.

---

# Refresh Workflow

A refresh is the process by which VDB exposes an updated reasoning-capacity package to downstream reasoning and later ingests refreshed reasoning assertions.

Conceptually:

```text
VDB current evidence state
        ↓
TEP-VDB refresh package
        ↓
RDGP or downstream reasoning system
        ↓
TEP-RDGP refreshed reasoning output
        ↓
VDB ingestion
        ↓
Updated reasoning-informed surfaces
```

VDB may identify that refresh is warranted.

VDB may package updated surfaces for downstream evaluation.

VDB may ingest refreshed reasoning outputs.

VDB does not perform the reasoning.

---

# Primary Surface Classes

Evidence Convergence Surfaces may be conceptually grouped into several classes.

## Primary Convergence Surface

A surface exposing geometry derived from raw producer assertions.

## Mixed-Modality Surface

A surface exposing geometry involving multiple raw evidence modalities.

## Reasoning-Informed Surface

A surface exposing geometry that includes preserved downstream reasoning assertions.

## Re-Entry Surface

A surface exposing geometry involving reasoning assertions that returned to VDB from a prior downstream reasoning pass.

## Audit Surface

A surface exposed for provenance, reconstruction, validation, or operator review.

These classes are conceptual.

The schema may later encode them more precisely.

---

# Surface Eligibility vs. Biological Importance

Surface eligibility is not biological importance.

A surface may be eligible because it satisfies deterministic structural rules.

This does not mean the surface is meaningful, significant, causal, actionable, or correct.

A surface provides an investigation opportunity.

RDGP determines whether statistical reasoning supports that opportunity.

Scientists and clinicians determine biological or clinical interpretation.

---

# What Evidence Convergence Surfaces May Claim

Evidence Convergence Surfaces may claim:

* this surface is geometry-derived
* this surface satisfies structural eligibility criteria
* this surface exposes defined convergence regions
* this surface discloses specified evidence strata
* this surface is traceable to topology and assertions
* this surface has a defined reasoning currency state
* this surface is current or stale relative to declared evidence and reasoning context
* this surface provides deterministic reasoning capacity

These are exposure claims.

They are not biological claims.

---

# What Evidence Convergence Surfaces May Not Claim

Evidence Convergence Surfaces must not claim:

* biological causality
* statistical significance
* clinical actionability
* disease mechanism
* hypothesis confirmation
* therapeutic relevance
* pathogenicity
* biological correctness
* prioritization

Those claims belong downstream.

---

# Relationship to RDGP

RDGP consumes Evidence Convergence Surfaces.

VDB provides:

* traceable evidence context
* structural geometry
* surface eligibility
* surface disclosure context
* evidence strata
* reasoning currency metadata

RDGP provides:

* statistical reasoning
* model-based evaluation
* prioritization
* confidence estimates
* downstream reasoning assertions

RDGP outputs may later re-enter VDB as preserved assertions.

This preserves the boundary:

```text
VDB exposes.
RDGP evaluates.
```

---

# Relationship to Future Reasoning Engines

Evidence Convergence Surfaces are not specific to RDGP.

Future reasoning systems may consume surfaces for:

* Bayesian integration
* enrichment analysis
* causal discovery
* graph reasoning
* topological reasoning
* machine learning
* clinical decision support
* future mathematical frameworks

Each reasoning system must remain outside VDB's preservation and exposure boundary.

If a reasoning system produces outputs that should be preserved, those outputs re-enter VDB as new assertions with their own provenance and epistemic status.

---

# Architectural Consequences

Evidence Convergence Surfaces establish several architectural invariants.

* Surfaces are geometry-derived.
* Surfaces expose; they do not interpret.
* Surface eligibility is structural.
* Surface disclosure is governed.
* Surface contents remain stratified.
* Surfaces preserve lineage to Geometry, Topology, and Assertion Records.
* Reasoning-informed surfaces preserve reasoning provenance.
* Surface generations remain historically valid.
* Reasoning currency must be tracked.
* Refresh is operator-visible and evidence-driven or method-driven.
* Downstream reasoning outputs re-enter VDB only as new assertions.

These invariants protect VDB from becoming a reasoning engine while enabling progressively richer investigation.

---

# Summary

Evidence Convergence Surfaces are the governed exposure layer of VDB.

They transform internal Convergence Geometry into deterministic reasoning capacity for downstream systems.

They define which structurally eligible regions may be queried, audited, compared, packaged, and handed to reasoning engines.

They do not determine what those regions mean.

Surfaces expose opportunity.

RDGP evaluates opportunity.

Scientists interpret evaluated evidence.

New reasoning outputs may re-enter VDB as preserved assertions, expanding future reasoning capacity without rewriting the past.

In this way, the Discovery Layer remains faithful to the Truth Layer while enabling the central emergent property of VDB:

```text
Preserved evidence accumulates.
Organization becomes richer.
Geometry becomes more expressive.
Surfaces expose greater reasoning capacity.
Downstream reasoning evaluates a subset.
New assertions return.
Future surfaces expand.
```

Discovery, within VDB, is therefore not interpretation.

It is governed, traceable, deterministic exposure of the scientific evidence substrate to the systems that reason beyond it.
